Love touches all of us, yet there is so much uncertainty surrounding it. We often wonder: where will we meet our soulmate? How long do people date for before they get married? Does our sexual life affect the quality of our relationships?

Thankfully, researchers at Stanford University were also curious about these questions, and they conducted a major study across the U.S., and published the survey responses in a dataset called "How Couples Meet and Stay Together."
To explore these recent trends in couples across America, read on!

How does frequency of sex influence quality of relationship?

We see that the respondents tended to rate their relationships excellent regardless of sex frequency, and there is not a clear trend. The data also suggest that very few couples will have a frequency of more than twice a week.

Could age differences within couples have an effect on marital status?

Here, we see that there is indeed a significant difference between couples who are many years apart in age and those that are closer in age in terms of marital status. Those with similar ages lie on the y=x dotted line.

To view detailed information, about the age and marital status of each respondent and the partner, hover over the circles on the visualization.

How long do people date before they get married?

On the right, each line segment represents the duration that a couple dated before getting married, plotted by age. To view the visualization in more detail, use the brushing component on the area chart below it.

One couple dated for thirty some years before finally getting married, but most other couples did not take nearly as long. Another couple went through meeting each other and getting married within a year. On average we see that most respondents dated for less than 6 years before getting married.
